Privacy Policy

How Aimee Skillin, LLC collects, uses, and protects your information on this website.

Effective Date: March 3, 2026

Overview

This Privacy Policy describes how Aimee Skillin, LLC ("we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, and shares information when you visit conotarypublic.com (the "Site"). We are committed to being transparent about our data practices and respecting your privacy.

By using this Site, you agree to the collection and use of information as described in this policy. If you do not agree, please discontinue use of the Site.

Information We Collect

Information Collected Automatically

When you visit our Site and have accepted cookies, Google Analytics 4 automatically collects certain information about your device and browsing activity, including:

  • Pages visited and time spent on each page
  • Your approximate geographic location (city/region level, derived from IP address)
  • Browser type, device type, and operating system
  • The website that referred you to our Site
  • Session duration and interaction events

This data is collected only if you have consented via the cookie banner on this Site. If you decline, no analytics data is collected.

Information You Provide Directly

If you contact us by phone or email (via links on this Site), you voluntarily provide information such as your name, phone number, and email address. This information is used solely to respond to your inquiry and arrange notary services.

How We Use Your Information

We use the information collected for the following purposes:

  • To understand how visitors use our Site and improve its content and usability
  • To respond to your inquiries and schedule notary appointments
  • To analyze traffic trends and optimize our services

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties.

Cookies & Tracking

Our Site uses cookies — small text files stored in your browser — to enable analytics functionality. We use a cookie consent mechanism to ensure analytics cookies are only placed after you explicitly accept.

We use the following types of cookies:

  • Essential cookies: Store your cookie consent preference (e.g., cookieConsent in localStorage). These are always active and do not track you.
  • Analytics cookies (consent-required): Set by Google Analytics 4 only after you accept. These help us understand aggregate traffic patterns.

You can withdraw your consent at any time by clearing your browser's localStorage or using the Google Analytics opt-out tool linked in the sidebar.

Third-Party Services

Google Analytics 4

We use Google Analytics 4 to understand how visitors interact with our Site. When you accept cookies, Google Analytics places cookies on your device and processes data on our behalf. Google may use this data in accordance with its own privacy policy.

  • Google Privacy Policy
  • Google Analytics Opt-Out Browser Add-on

Google Fonts

This Site loads fonts (Playfair Display and Source Sans 3) from Google Fonts. When your browser requests these fonts, Google receives your IP address and browser information. Google's use of this data is governed by Google's Privacy Policy. No cookies are placed by Google Fonts.

Google Calendar Appointments

Our "Book Now" link directs you to Google Calendar's appointment scheduling service. If you use this service, Google's privacy policy governs the information collected during that interaction.

Affiliate Links

Some links on our Resources page are affiliate links. If you click an affiliate link and make a purchase, we may earn a commission at no additional cost to you. Those third-party websites have their own privacy policies, which we encourage you to review.

Data Retention

Analytics data collected through Google Analytics is retained for 14 months by default, after which it is automatically deleted from Google's systems. Contact information you provide directly (e.g., by email) is retained only as long as necessary to fulfill the purpose for which it was shared.

Your Rights (California Residents — CCPA)

If you are a California resident, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) grants you specific rights regarding your personal information.

Under the CCPA, you have the right to:

  • Know what personal information we have collected about you and how it is used
  • Delete personal information we have collected from you (subject to certain exceptions)
  • Opt out of the sale of your personal information
  • Non-discrimination — we will not discriminate against you for exercising your privacy rights

We do not sell personal information. To exercise your rights to know or delete, please contact us using the information below. We will respond within 45 days.

Children's Privacy

This Site is not directed to children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us and we will promptly delete it.

Data Security

We take reasonable precautions to protect the information on this Site. However, no method of transmission over the Internet is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the "Effective Date" at the top of this page. We encourage you to review this page periodically. Continued use of the Site after any changes constitutes your acceptance of the updated policy.
